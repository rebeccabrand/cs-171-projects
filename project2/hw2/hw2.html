2. Infographic Design Critique


This infographic poses the question, "Which roles have the highest earning at Google?" The audience might be future Google employees who are interested in various roles at the company, or just a general population who are interested in how major technology companies compensate their staff. With these conditions in mind, the infographic employs quantitative data on salary ranges and categorical data on job types. The salary ranges are visually encoded within the central pie chart as proportional to the average range of top salaries. The job type data is visually encoded as color variations within the pie chart, and both data types have text markers outside the central image that explain their associations. Personally, I think the infographic is overly busy with information, especially with the overlapping colored sections in the middle. It is difficult to understand each slice's relationship to the whole, which leads the viewer to rely on the written components of the infographic instead. Additionally, the human symbols help identify associations within the pie chart, but are otherwise distracting and inconsistent for seemingly little reason.
